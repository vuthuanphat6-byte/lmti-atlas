# 003 Why AI Forgets

AI forgets in a strange way.

It may give a good answer.

It may find the right file.

It may explain a bug clearly.

Then the next task begins, and much of that understanding is gone.

The text may still exist somewhere.

The chat may be saved.

The logs may be stored.

But the understanding is not owned by the system.

It was produced for a moment.

Then it dissolved back into text.

This is different from how people work.

When we learn a project, we do not only save transcripts.

We update our internal model.

We become a little harder to confuse next time.

We know where not to look.

We know which paths are likely noise.

We know which files deserve attention first.

So maybe forgetting is not the absence of stored data.

Maybe forgetting is the failure to turn experience into reusable structure.

That is the question LMTI cares about.

Can an AI agent preserve useful understanding locally?

Can it reduce repeated exploration?

Can it stop paying the same cognitive cost again and again?

I do not know yet.

That is why this project has to be measured.
