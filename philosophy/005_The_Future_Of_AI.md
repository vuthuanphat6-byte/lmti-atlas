# 005 The Future Of AI

I do not want to say what the future of AI is.

That is too easy.

It is better to ask what still feels missing.

Why does context disappear so quickly?

Why does every task start so close to zero?

Why does an agent search files it has already learned are not important?

Why does useful reasoning fail to become part of the next task?

Maybe bigger models will solve some of this.

Maybe longer context windows will help.

Maybe better tools will help.

But I suspect there is another layer.

Not a replacement for models.

Not a replacement for tools.

A layer that organizes understanding.

Humans do not become useful only because we can read more text at once.

We become useful because we build models of the world.

We compress experience into judgment.

We remember what deserves attention.

We forget noise.

We carry uncertainty.

We revise our beliefs when evidence changes.

Could AI systems do something similar around software projects?

Could an agent build a durable map of a codebase?

Could that map reduce unnecessary thinking?

Could it make collaboration calmer, safer and less repetitive?

These are the questions I want LMTI to hold.

Not answers yet.

Questions worth testing.
