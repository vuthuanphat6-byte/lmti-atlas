# Code Of Conduct

LMTI contributors are expected to keep collaboration respectful, practical, and
security-minded.

## Expected Behavior

- Be clear, kind, and constructive.
- Assume good intent while still reviewing code and claims carefully.
- Keep discussions focused on evidence, tests, docs, and user impact.
- Respect privacy boundaries and avoid sharing secrets or private project data.
- Help new contributors understand the current local-alpha scope.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, threats, insults, or discriminatory language.
- Publishing another person's private information.
- Posting real secrets, tokens, credentials, or private deployment details.
- Pressuring maintainers to bypass security review.
- Repeatedly claiming unsupported capabilities after correction.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit, hide, or remove comments and contributions that violate
this code. Severe or repeated violations may lead to temporary or permanent loss
of participation privileges.
